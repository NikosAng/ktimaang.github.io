The Mercati Family: A Legacy Since 1538

The Mercati family's rich history spans nearly five centuries, beginning with their arrival on the picturesque island of Zakynthos in 1538. Originally hailing from Florence, the Mercati family quickly established themselves as prominent members of the island's aristocracy under Venetian rule.

Noble Beginnings

Upon their arrival in Zakynthos, the Mercati family's noble status was recognized and their name was inscribed in the Libro d'Oro, the official register of the island's aristocracy. This marked the beginning of their influential presence on the island.

Establishing Roots

In 1818, the Mercati family built their first house on what would become their sprawling estate. The location was carefully chosen for its fertile soil, perfect for cultivating olive trees. This decision laid the foundation for the family's long-standing tradition in olive oil production.

A Legacy in Olive Oil

The family's commitment to olive oil production reached new heights in 1840 when Mighelle Mercati authored a groundbreaking olive oil textbook. This publication not only showcased the family's expertise but also contributed significantly to the advancement of olive oil production techniques in the region.

Continuing the Tradition

Today, the Mercati family continues to honor their rich heritage. The estate, now over 200 years old, still stands as a testament to the family's enduring legacy. The tradition of producing high-quality olive oil, started by their ancestors, remains at the heart of the family's pursuits.
